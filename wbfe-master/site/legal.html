Legal Authority


The Indigenous Nations Tribal Reserve (INTR) was tribally chartered in 2001 for the purpose of economically benefiting low income indigenous communities inside and outside the United States. INTR serves as the parent for the INTR Credit Program (INFCP) and the White Buffalo Co-op, an Oklahoma Limited Cooperative Association (LCA). INTR and it’s subsidiaries are US Department of Treasury certified Community Development Entities (CDEs). INTR’s Federal charter obligation is to “safeguard and define” the rights of intertribal constituents.

Federal Acts, including the Native American Business Development, Trade Promotion, & Tourism Act (NABDA, 25 U.S.C. 4301 et seq.), allow for the formation of the INTR. State and Federal Acts and Laws, including the Oklahoma Indian Welfare Act (OIWA, 25 U.S.C 501 et seq.) and Limited Cooperative Association Act allow for the formation of the White Buffalo Co-op.

Credit and cooperatives to create ethical businesses


As Community Development Entities, INTR and its subsidiaries can operate within Opportunity Zones and accredited investors are eligible for New Market Tax Credit benefits. INTR is eligible for the USDA Rural Business Enterprise Grant (RBEG) program assistance and in 2002 the USDA authorised several INTR subsidiaries to operate a revolving loan fund. INTR can also issue tribal bonds under the IRS and Indian Finance Act, U.S. Department of Interior programs. INTR has established a Transnational Branch Credit Program in Lichtenstein and a Reservation Branch Credit Program for indigenous tribes and bands to launch their own INTR-backed credit programs. The White Buffalo Co-op allows anyone to join the Co-op and work on or launch businesses through its subsidiary, E-COUP L.C.A. (Limited Cooperative Association), the incubator for LLCs funded through Reg D private placements to accredited investors. In contrast, the INFCP incubates LLCs funded by the INTR. The Tribal Reserve issues a transferable commodity as a programmable distributed ledger technology asset used by INTR and its LLCs, referred to as TRAK$>Credits.
